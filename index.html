Fiordland

Fiordland, located in the southwestern corner of the south island of New Zealand, is home of famous Milford and Doubtful Sounds as well as many world famous walking tracks. Fiordland is one of the most dramatic and beautiful parts of New Zealand and is renowned as a Sightseeing and Walking Capital. This is one of the wettest places in the world and this rain contributes to the area's beauty. Waterfalls tumble down steep mountains to the sea and rain nourishes rich beech forest. Stunning landscapes range from hill country to rugged mountains and lush rainforests. The climate offers summer and ski options providing year round attractions, contrasting with bustling cafes and shopping possibilities, and remote wildernesses and solitude. Activities are centred on four main areas of Fiordland: Te Anau Manapouri Milford Sound Doubtful Sound Fiordland National Park has a world wide reputation for its walking tracks which are among the best in the world, and some of the finest scenery Fiordland has to offer can be seen from the following tracks: Milford Track One of New Zealand's most popular walks, the 53.5 km Milford Track was once described as the finest walk in the world Routeburn The Routeburn track traverses 33km of Mount Aspiring and Fiordland National Parks and extends between the head of Lake Wakatipu and the Milford road The Kepler Track A 60 kilometre circular track travelling through spectacular scenery in Fiordland National Park. Visitors to Fiordland have a wide range of attractions to choose from. You can admire Fiordland National Park's scenic splendour from the air, land and sea and take part in all of the following activities: Walking/Tramping Fishing Scenic Flights Cruises Kayaking Farm Tours Destination Fiordland is the Regional Tourism Organisation for the Fiordland region. Walking/Tramping In addition to the famous Great Walks and multi-day hiking tracks that have earned Fiordland the reputation of the "Walking Capital of the World", day and half day walks include the Wildlife Park and local walks at Manapouri, as well as at Lake Hauroko and Monowai and numerous stopping points and short walks on the famous Milford Road Fishing Brown and rainbow trout thrive in Fiordland's lakes and rivers. Trolling for fish on the lakes of Fiordland and fly fishing in Fiordland's rivers is very popular Scenic Flights Enjoy the scenery of Fiordland from a different perspective. Flights depart from Te Anau and Milford Sound and you can take to the air in a fixed wing plane, float plane, or helicopter. Cruises Cruise the idyllic waters of the lakes and sounds and admire the magnificent scenery that these unique regions have to offer. Kayaking Fiordland is a popular destination for kayakers. What better way of enjoying Fiordland's dramatic scenery than kayaking among its many fiord's, lakes, waterfalls and sheer rock faces
